# Thermal pRF fMRI Experiment — Analysis Briefing

## Experiment Overview

Continuous triangular-wave thermal stimulation delivered via a 5-zone TCS II thermode during fMRI. The goal is **thermotopic mapping** of the human brain: identifying cortical maps that track stimulus temperature magnitude from mild to intense, analogous to tonotopic maps in audition or retinotopic maps in vision.

Two conditions are compared:
- **Non-TGI**: uniform temperature across adjacent zones (control)
- **TGI**: alternating warm/cool zones to evoke the Thermal Grill Illusion

|-----------|-------|

## Stimulation Design

### Waveform

Bipolar triangular wave applied continuously throughout each run:

```
Temperature
    ↑
 47.5°C ──      ╱╲          ╱╲
              ╱    ╲      ╱    ╲
 30.0°C ──  ╱      ╲    ╱      ╲    ╱
                     ╲  ╱        ╲  ╱
 12.5°C ──            ╲╱          ╲╱

            |── 80s ──|── 80s ──|
              1 cycle    1 cycle
```

- **Cycle duration**: 80 s (one full bipolar period: 0 → +Δ → 0 → −Δ → 0)
- **Half-period**: 40 s (one sweep direction)
- **Max delta**: 17.5°C from 30°C baseline
- **Temperature range**: 12.5°C – 47.5°C
- **Ramp rate**: ~0.875°C/s
- **Cycles per run**: 8.5 (8 full + 1 half)
- **Stimulation duration**: 680 s

### Block Structure (each run)

1. **Baseline** (30 s): all zones held at 30°C
2. **Stimulation** (680 s): 8.5 cycles of triangular wave
3. **Baseline** (30 s): all zones held at 30°C

### Spatial Masks (5-zone thermode)

Each mask defines how the scalar delta waveform maps to the 5 zones. Zone temperature = 30 + (mask_sign × delta).

**Non-TGI masks** (uniform temperature on 2 adjacent zones):

| Mask | Zone 1 | Zone 2 | Zone 3 | Zone 4 | Zone 5 |
|------|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| P1_W | +1 | +1 | 0 | 0 | 0 |
| P1_C | −1 | −1 | 0 | 0 | 0 |
| P3_W | 0 | 0 | +1 | +1 | 0 |
| P3_C | 0 | 0 | −1 | −1 | 0 |

**TGI masks** (alternating warm/cool for thermal grill illusion):

| Mask | Zone 1 | Zone 2 | Zone 3 | Zone 4 | Zone 5 |
|------|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| TGI_1 | +1 | −1 | +1 | −1 | 0 |
| TGI_2 | −1 | +1 | −1 | +1 | 0 |

Zone 5 is always neutral (held at baseline). When delta is positive, +1 zones warm and −1 zones cool; when delta swings negative, the pattern reverses. In TGI masks, adjacent zones always move in opposite directions, creating the thermal grill illusion.

Post-block perceptual ratings (0–100):

### Pre-computed Design Matrices

Generated by `generate_design_matrix.py`. All regressors are fully deterministic (no jittering) — computed from config parameters alone.

**GLM regressors** (HRF-convolved using SPM canonical double-gamma HRF, sampled at TR):

1. **`stim_boxcar`** — 1 during stimulation, 0 during baselines. Captures overall BOLD response to thermal stimulation.
2. **`delta_centered`** — triangular waveform amplitude, mean-centered within the stimulation period (orthogonal to boxcar). Captures parametric modulation by temperature magnitude.
3. **`delta_derivative`** — dΔ/dt, rate of temperature change, mean-centered. Captures response to temperature transitions.

Regressor correlations are near-zero (r ≈ 0.01) due to mean-centering.

**pRF stimulus aperture** (sampled at TR):
- 5 columns: commanded temperature per zone at each volume
- Provided both unconvolved and HRF-convolved
- Output formats: TSV (nilearn), .mat (SPM), .npz (numpy), JSON metadata, PNG plot

## Analysis Considerations

### pRF Modelling — Thermotopic Mapping

The primary analysis goal is to identify **thermotopic maps**: cortical regions where voxels are systematically tuned to specific temperature magnitudes, from mild (~30°C) to intense (~47.5°C warm or ~12.5°C cool).

The pRF model treats the stimulus as the **temperature magnitude delivered across all zones together** — the continuously varying delta waveform — not individual zone responses. The triangular wave sweeps through the full temperature range at a constant rate, analogous to a drifting bar in visual retinotopy. Voxels that respond preferentially to specific temperature magnitudes will show peak activation at predictable phases of the triangle wave.

The warm-first and cool-first runs provide complementary phase coverage, helping disambiguate thermotopic tuning from hemodynamic lag.

### GLM Analysis

The three orthogonal regressors allow separating:
- **Boxcar**: overall activation during stimulation vs rest (regions responsive to any thermal input)
- **Delta**: voxels whose BOLD signal tracks temperature magnitude (thermotopic tuning)
- **Derivative**: voxels sensitive to the rate/direction of temperature change (warming vs cooling transitions)

Nuisance regressors (motion parameters, aCompCor, drift/high-pass filter) must be added after fMRI preprocessing.

### TGI vs Non-TGI Contrast

The critical experimental comparison. Both conditions use identical waveform parameters (same delta, same timing). The only difference is the spatial pattern on the thermode:
- **Non-TGI**: adjacent zones receive the same temperature — a simple thermal stimulus
- **TGI**: adjacent zones receive opposite temperatures — warm next to cool, producing the thermal grill illusion (paradoxical burning sensation)

Contrasting these conditions reveals brain regions involved in generating or processing the illusory burning percept.

### Run 4 Data Loss

Run 4 (TGI cool-first) is incomplete due to thermode failure after ~2.5 cycles (~200 s of stimulation out of 680 s). Analysis options:
- Exclude run 4 entirely
- Model only the available portion (first 2.5 cycles) with an appropriately truncated design matrix
- Include with censoring of post-failure volumes

### Thermode QC

The 10 Hz actual-vs-commanded temperature recordings enable:
- Verification that the thermode tracked the waveform accurately
- Identification of cycles with poor tracking (ramp flags, temperature error)
- Potential use of actual temperatures (rather than commanded) as regressors if tracking was imperfect
